LOWTAPER
A low taper is a haircut style where the hair gradually becomes shorter toward the sides and back, starting very close to the neckline and ears. Unlike a fade, a low taper does not cut the hair down to the skin; instead, it creates a smooth and natural transition in length. This style keeps most of the hair intact on the top and upper sides, giving a clean and neat appearance without looking too bold.
Mullet
A mullet haircut where the sides and back are cut with a low fade, starting close to the neckline.
BUZZCUT
A buzz cut is a very short hairstyle where the hair is cut evenly close to the scalp using clippers. It is known for its clean, simple, and low-maintenance look. Because the hair length is uniform, the buzz cut highlights facial features and gives a sharp appearance. This haircut is popular among people who prefer practicality, comfort, and a neat style, making it suitable for both casual and professional settings.
TAPER PADE
A taper padé is a haircut style where the hair gradually shortens along the sides and back, creating a smooth and natural transition from longer hair on top to shorter hair near the neckline. Unlike a skin fade, the taper padé keeps some length, giving a clean and polished look without being too sharp. This hairstyle is popular for its neat appearance, versatility, and suitability for school, work, and formal occasions.
